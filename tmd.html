"Cristal"

Motion After Effects Visual System

A project developed as part of the "Typography in digital media" course in the second year of my undergraduate degree. To develop this project, I chose the poem "Cristal" by Haroldo de Campos (1958) because of how it explores the tension between structure and raw emotion. The author’s approach allows for a deep reflection on the transition from clarity to hunger, creating a dynamic sensory experience.

Clarity and fracture — "Cristal" investigates the tension between transparency and opacity, between what is seen and what is refracted.

Developed through After Effects and layered physical processes, this project constructs a language of surfaces — reflective, fragile, precise. The material becomes the metaphor.
